Gluten-Free, Dairy-Free Pumpkin Pie

Description

Pumpkin pie has been a staple of my family since I was a little girl. In fact, I think it may be my favorite pie! I love shoving it in my mouth every Thanksgiving. Am I a turkey?? Because I'm STUFFED.

I've recently gone gluten-free, dairy-free. While that's good for my health, it's bad for recipes. Did you know it's really hard to bake without gluten OR dairy?? Well, this is a delicious option that I've fallen in love with. Homemade and based off of my mother's recipe! But now it's mine :)

Ingredients

Pie Crust

  • 2 cups gluten-free bread flour (include xanthan gum)
  • 2 tablespoons light brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup dairy-free butter
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • ice water

Pie

  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup light brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup dairy-free butter, softened
  • 1 tablespoon gluten-free bread flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/8 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 3 large eggs, beaten
  • 2 cups (1 15oz can) pumpkin
  • 3/4 cups light coconut milk

Steps

Crust Instructions

  1. Whisk flour and sugar until fully combined.
  2. Cut the butter into the mixture with either the back of a fork or a pastry cutter until sandy and crumbly.
  3. Add the beaten egg and mix well. Mixture will be crumbly, but yellow.
  4. Fold ice cold water tablespoon by tablespoon into the mixture until a dough holds together. Better to have a wetter dough than a drier dough.
  5. Shape dough into a disc and roll out the dough between two pieces of parchment paper or on a slipmat until the dough is slightly larger than your pie tin.
  6. Flip the dough into your pie tin and shape it. If it cracks or breaks, you can mend it and shape it back into place.
  7. For this recipe, par bake the crust for 12 minutes at 425F. It will bubble in the center, this hot air can be pressed out or you can use pie weights.

Pie Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the sugars, flour, salt, and spices.
  2. In a separate bowl, beat together the eggs, vanilla, pumpkin, and coconut milk.
  3. Whisk into the dry ingredients. (For best flavor, cover and refrigerate the filling overnight before baking.)
  4. Using a deep-dish pie shell, crimp the edges above the rim; this will give you a little extra headroom to hold the filling when it expands in the oven.
  5. Preheat oven to 400˚F.
  6. When the oven is hot, place the pie pan on a baking sheet to catch any drips.
  7. Pour the filling into the par baked pie shell.
  8. Bake for 45-50 minutes, until the filling is set 2” in from the edge. (The center should still be wobbly.)
  9. Remove the pie from the oven and cool on a rack; the center will finish cooking through as the pie sits.
